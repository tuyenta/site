I am a PhD student at Telecom ParisTec (France) working with Prof. Van-Tam Nguyen at the Laboratory for the Treatment and Communication of Information (LTCI). My research interests lie in algorithmic learning, game theory, and their applications to networks.

As an undergrad, I majored in electrical engineering. After graduating in 2008, I enrolled in the graduate program of the Electronics and Telecomunication Department of University of Engineering and Technology-VNUH (Vietnam). While there, I worked on high speed comminication systems with Anh-Vu Trinh and I got my M.Sc. in Electronics in 2010. My interests then shifted to applied mathematics and signal processing, so I started my internship with Nobutaka Ono at National Information Institue (NII), Japan on acoustic processing in Apr. 2014 and then the thesis work with Van-Tam Nguyen in Nov. 2014. During my Ph.D., I worked on the applications of game theory and deep learning to wireless networks.
